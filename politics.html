Politics of Welfare explores the complexities of social welfare policies, their historical development, and their impact on society. It examines how governments design welfare programs, the political ideologies that shape them, and the ongoing debates about their effectiveness.

AThe book provides an in-depth analysis of welfare systems across different countries, highlighting the tension between social justice and economic sustainability. It delves into issues like poverty, unemployment benefits, healthcare, and education, discussing how political parties, interest groups, and economic forces influence welfare policies. By drawing on case studies and real-world examples, Politics of Welfare offers a critical perspective on the challenges of creating fair and effective social safety nets in modern societies.
