An artist's book is a unique and innovative form of artistic expression where the book itself is considered a work of art, rather than just a vessel for text or images. Unlike traditional books, which primarily serve to convey information or tell a story, artist books emphasize creative design, experimental structures, and conceptual depth, often blurring the line between literature and visual art.

Artist books focus on the visual and conceptual experience, often blending fine art, design, and storytelling to create a unique and immersive form of artistic expression. Unlike traditional books, which primarily serve as vessels for text or imagery, artist books treat the book itself as an art object, challenging conventional notions of format, structure, and meaning. These works can take on a variety of forms, ranging from handmade, limited-edition pieces to experimental, sculptural creations that defy the typical book format.Throughout history, many influential artists, including Marcel Duchamp, Ed Ruscha, and Dieter Roth, have explored this medium, using it to push the boundaries of art and communication. The artist book movement gained prominence in the 20th century, particularly within avant-garde circles, where it served as a platform for radical artistic and political ideas. These books often incorporate unconventional materials, innovative bindings, and interactive elements, encouraging viewers to engage with them beyond passive reading..
