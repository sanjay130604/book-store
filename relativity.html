Albert Einstein’s The Theory of Relativity revolutionized physics by introducing the concepts of special and general relativity. It fundamentally changed our understanding of space, time, and gravity.

The theory explains how time and space are not absolute but relative, depending on the observer’s motion. Special relativity introduced the famous equation E=mc², showing the relationship between energy and mass. General relativity expanded on this by describing gravity as the curvature of spacetime caused by massive objects. Einstein’s work has had profound implications in cosmology, astrophysics, and modern technology, shaping everything from GPS systems to our understanding of black holes and the expanding universe.
